House Point Rules

To ensure the House Cup Challenge remains fair, transparent and motivating for everyone, teachers award or deduct points according to the guidelines below. These rules help celebrate positive actions and accomplishments while encouraging accountability and growth.

Positive Contributions

  • Major achievements (50–100 pts): Winning competitions, science fairs, debates, sports championships or exceptional community service.
  • Academic excellence (20–50 pts): Outstanding performance on exams or projects, demonstrating leadership in group work or tutoring peers.
  • Citizenship and service (10–30 pts): Acts of kindness, volunteering, mentorship, positive role‑modelling and contributions to school events.
  • Participation (5–15 pts): Joining house events, sports days, competitions, cultural activities or club meetings.

Negative Consequences

  • Minor infractions (−5 pts): Tardiness, missing homework, lack of participation or disruptive behaviour.
  • Major infractions (−10 to −30 pts): Academic dishonesty, disrespectful conduct, damaging property or repeated rule violations.

Points should be awarded or deducted fairly and promptly. Teachers are encouraged to provide a brief reason for each adjustment so students understand what behaviour is being recognised. Remember, the goal of the House Cup is to promote teamwork, excellence and school spirit for everyone!
